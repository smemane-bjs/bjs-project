About Us

Bharatiya Jain Sanghatana (BJS) has been in the forefront of addressing national concerns in the areas of Disaster Response, Social Development and Educational Initiatives since its establishment, in the year 1985. BJS has its head office in Pune, is a non-political, non-profit, professionally managed non-governmental organization (NGO) a nationwide footprint, working towards the benefit of all.

BJS has participated in disaster response activities in all major disasters since Latur earthquake in the year 1993. A major earthquake of Latur – Osmanabad in Maharashtra. There onwards, BJS worked in Jabalpur (Madhya Pradesh) earthquake – 1996, Gujarat earthquake – 2001, Akola (Maharashtra) floods – 2002, Andaman & Nicobar tsunami – 2005, Jammu & Kashmir earthquake – 2005, Bihar floods – 2008, and Maharashtra drought – 2013 & 2016.

Through drought relief program, “Sujalam Sufalam –Maharashtra” we aim to achieve our goal of water abundance for all.

BJS has successfully executed Sujalam Sufalam initiative in Buldhana district within a time span of 4 months, started from March-June 2018. Therefore, the success of this initiative could evidently be seen working for the entire district progressing towards water abundance. This year BJS and Maharashtra government has signed a reconciliation agreement on 18 June, 2018 to implement the initiative for Latur, Osmanabad, Akola, and Washim along with Buldhana.

Aiming to transform Maharashtra into a water abundant state, and completely emancipate the state from drought related challenges. By selecting one drought-prone zone at a time and redeveloping its existing catchment area to increase the water harvesting capacity within a time span of one year. For this projection considering the availability of M.I. tanks, flush ponds, village & farm ponds, drainage widening & deepening etc. was considered. Also, measurement and evaluation of availability of different types of structures and the potential of increase in water storage capacity is planned with district administrators.

We ensure active and moderate participation of multiple shareholders like Maharashtra government, Zilla & Talluka officials, Public representatives, General public, Farmers, JCB/Pokland machine owners , NGOs, CSR companies and Media. Creating a positive and optimistic atmosphere in an entire region and making this the movement of the great public participation. Monitoring and documenting the activities by promptly coordinating between all the stakeholders and public

We are also working in the areas of education & various relief Projects. This organization is not influenced by any political party. It keeps aside the barriers of caste, community, religion, and political parties. BJS works for the social causes like rehabilitation of the children of the farmers who committed a suicide in Maharashtra. drought relief and other disaster management.

Our Founder - President

Mr. Shantilal Muttha is a philanthropist, social entrepreneur and a self-made man. One of the eminent pioneers in social work, and the founder of (BJS) Bharatiya Jain Sanghatana who is committed to his work of social development in India from last 30 years. He rose from extremely humble origins, he realized the potential of entrepreneurship to sow new seeds for positive change and transformation in the society.

He graduated from the BMCC (Brihan Maharashtra College of Commerce), Pune, gave up his flourishing real estate business at the young age of 31, to pursue his heart`s calling- to establish the Bharatiya Jain Sanghatana (BJS) in the year 1985. A non-political, non-profit, secular organization with a vision "To collectively contribute towards social welfare through identifying the scope and bridging the gaps by empowering masses." Under Mr. Muttha`s strong leadership, vigilant business acumen and splendid interpersonal skills he has established a strong team of enthusiasts, influencers, volunteers and a fine network to build a strong foundation for a great cause.
